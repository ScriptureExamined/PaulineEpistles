The Two Gospels

A study of the distinction between the gospel of the circumcision and the gospel of the uncircumcision.

A Text-Driven Distinction
What if Scripture presents not one uniform message, but two distinct administrations with different audiences and expectations?
Focus
The distinction between Israel’s program and the body of Christ.
Question
Are all passages speaking to the same audience, or do they reflect separate divine administrations?
Method
Careful comparison of passages, terminology, and context—especially in Paul’s epistles.
Aim
To clarify distinctions that resolve apparent contradictions in Scripture.

Overview

What This Study Examines

Much of modern teaching assumes that all of Scripture presents a single, unified message without distinction. This study examines whether that assumption holds up when the text is carefully compared.

By examining the differences between the gospel proclaimed to the circumcision and that given to Paul for the nations, this work explores whether Scripture itself presents two distinct administrations.

Special attention is given to passages that are often blended together, resulting in confusion, contradiction, or forced interpretation.

Inside the Study

What You Will Find

This work is written to be examined alongside Scripture with careful attention to audience and context.

Two Gospels: A clear distinction between the message to Israel and the message given through Paul.

Defined Audiences: Careful attention to who is being addressed in each passage.

Doctrinal Separation: Clarifying differences that are often merged into a single system.

Text First: Letting Scripture establish its own divisions without forcing harmony where none is stated.

For the Reader

Who This Study Is For

This book is written for readers willing to question assumptions and examine Scripture closely. It is for those who have encountered contradictions in teaching and want to resolve them through the text itself.

Agreement is not required. Careful comparison is.
