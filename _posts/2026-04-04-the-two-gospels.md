In *The Will of God*, I focused on a single question:

Is God’s will something uncertain, or something already in operation?

By following the language of Scripture, especially in the writings of Paul, the conclusion was not left open. God’s will is described as active, deliberate, and effective. Not waiting. Not dependent. Operating.

That foundation raises another question.

If the will of God is already in operation, then how are we to understand the different messages presented in Scripture?

This is where my current work begins.

*The Two Gospels* examines a distinction that is often overlooked:
the difference between what Peter proclaimed and what Paul proclaimed.

Scripture speaks of:
“the gospel of the circumcision” and
“the gospel of the uncircumcision” (Galatians 2:7)

These are not casual phrases. They are specific, and they deserve to be examined as they are written.

This upcoming work is not an argument for a system.
It is a careful, text-driven examination of what is actually being said.

The goal is simple:
to follow the language of Scripture where it leads, without forcing it to fit what we expect.
