Books and Studies Rooted in the Text

This page gathers the current and upcoming works published through this site. Each book is written with the same aim: to examine Scripture carefully, preserve its distinctions, and follow the text with clarity and precision.

Featured Title

The Two Gospels

A study of the distinction between the gospel of the circumcision and the gospel of the uncircumcision.

Much of modern teaching treats the gospel as one uniform message throughout Scripture. This study examines whether that assumption holds up when the text is carefully compared.

By comparing the message committed to Peter with the message committed to Paul, this book considers whether Scripture presents two distinct administrations with different audiences, expectations, and promises.

Particular attention is given to the gospel of the circumcision, the gospel of the uncircumcision, Israel’s place in the kingdom, and the distinct calling of the body of Christ.

Available Study

The Will of God

A study of Paul’s language concerning divine intention, operation, and purpose.

Much of modern teaching presents the will of God as something uncertain, hidden, or dependent on human discovery. This study examines whether that idea can actually be sustained from Paul’s writings.

By following Paul’s language closely, the book considers how Scripture speaks about God’s will—not as fragile or pending, but as deliberate, active, and effective.

Particular attention is given to the wording of the text, including key terms that are often flattened in translation and therefore misunderstood in doctrine.

Shared Foundation

What Unites These Works

Each study is built on the same foundation: careful reading, defined terms, and conclusions drawn from the text itself.

Scripture First: Each study begins with the wording of Scripture itself rather than a system imposed on top of it.

Defined Terms: Doctrinal conclusions depend on language. Key words are handled carefully, not loosely.

Real Distinctions: When the text distinguishes between things, those distinctions are preserved and examined.

Serious Study: These books are written for readers who want more than summaries and are meant to be tested against the text.
