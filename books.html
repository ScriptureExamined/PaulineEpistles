Books

Books and Studies Rooted in the Text

This page gathers the current and upcoming works published through this site. Each book is written with the same aim: to examine Scripture carefully, preserve its distinctions, and follow the text with clarity and precision.

These are not casual reflections or generalized devotional summaries. They are focused studies intended for readers who want to slow down, examine what is written, and think carefully about the language of Scripture.

Some books will address a single doctrinal issue in depth. Others will trace broader scriptural themes. All are built on the same foundation: Scripture first, careful distinctions, and conclusions drawn from the text itself.

Featured Title

The Two Gospels

A study of the distinction between the gospel of the circumcision and the gospel of the uncircumcision.

Much of modern teaching treats the gospel as one uniform message throughout Scripture. This study examines whether that assumption holds up when the text is carefully compared.

By comparing the message committed to Peter with the message committed to Paul, this book considers whether Scripture presents two distinct administrations with different audiences, expectations, and promises.

Particular attention is given to the gospel of the circumcision, the gospel of the uncircumcision, Israel’s place in the kingdom, and the distinct calling of the body of Christ.

Shared Foundation

What Unites These Works

Each study is built on the same foundation: careful reading, defined terms, and conclusions drawn from the text itself.

Scripture First: Each study begins with the wording of Scripture itself rather than a system imposed on top of it.

Defined Terms: Doctrinal conclusions depend on language. Key words are handled carefully, not loosely.

Real Distinctions: When the text distinguishes between things, those distinctions are preserved and examined.

Serious Study: These books are written for readers who want more than summaries and are meant to be tested against the text.

For the Reader

Read With the Text Open

These books are intended to be used alongside Scripture. They are written to direct attention back to the text, not away from it.

Readers are encouraged to examine the passages, consider the wording, and test every argument carefully. The goal is not to demand agreement, but to make careful examination unavoidable.

Outline

The Two Gospels

This preview provides a brief outline of the study’s direction and emphasis.

Introduction: Why the common idea of one uniform gospel message needs to be tested against Scripture.

The Gospel of the Circumcision: The message connected to Israel, the kingdom, and Jesus as the promised Messiah.

The Gospel of the Uncircumcision: Paul’s distinct gospel concerning the body of Christ and justification apart from Israel’s program.

Two Administrations: How Scripture presents different divine operations for Israel and the nations.

Israel’s Preeminence: Why Israel retains preeminence in the kingdom promises, while Paul relinquishes his Israel advantage in the body of Christ.

The Body of Christ: How the uncircumcision is identified with the body of Christ rather than Israel’s covenant promises.

Conclusion: A call to let Scripture maintain its own distinctions instead of blending what God has separated.
